The Idea

Trusted Intent is built on a simple truth: leadership is not about authority. It is about responsibility. People follow leaders whose motives they trust, whose standards they believe, and whose example holds steady when the pressure rises.

Through stories from combat, corporate leadership, crisis response, mentorship, marriage, and parenting, this platform explores what trustworthy leadership actually looks like and why trust is built long before it is tested.

At work

For leaders who want practical clarity on culture, standards, accountability, decision-making, and building teams that perform with trust.

At home

Because leadership is not confined to formations, offices, or boardrooms. The people closest to us experience our character most directly.

When it matters

Pressure does not create leadership. It reveals it. Trusted intent is formed in daily choices long before the decisive moment arrives.

“People do not truly follow leaders because of rank, authority, or position. They follow leaders whose intent they trust.”

The Book

Trusted Intent is a story-driven leadership book about why people follow some leaders and merely comply with others. It is not a formula, a checklist, or a theory of heroic leadership. It is a practical exploration of trustworthy leadership through real stories of responsibility, example, sacrifice, humility, wisdom, and failure.

The lessons are drawn from military command, enterprise leadership, and family life, but the message is broader than any one field. This is a book about leadership itself.

About Blake Glass

Blake Glass is a Brigadier General and enterprise operations leader with nearly three decades of leadership experience across combat operations, crisis environments, strategic decision-making, and corporate development.

His background spans military command, Joint Staff service, large-scale operational leadership, and private-sector execution, along with the most important leadership arena of all: life at home as a husband and father.
